How is Pneumonia Diagnosed?

Pneumonia can be diagnosed through a combination of medical history, physical examination, and specific tests. The doctor will start by listening to your lungs using a stethoscope to check for abnormal sounds like crackling or wheezing. This is often followed by a chest X-ray to identify areas of infection in the lungs.

Additional tests may include blood tests to detect infections and sputum tests to analyze the mucus. In some cases, a CT scan or bronchoscopy may be recommended for a detailed view of the lungs. Early and accurate diagnosis is crucial for effective treatment and recovery.
